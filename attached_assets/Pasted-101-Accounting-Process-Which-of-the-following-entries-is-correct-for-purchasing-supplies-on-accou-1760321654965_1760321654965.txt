103. (Corporate Accounting)
What is the impact of declaring and paying cash dividends on the accounting equation?
A. Increase assets, increase equity
B. Decrease assets, increase liabilities
C. Decrease assets, decrease equity
D. Increase liabilities, increase assets

Answer: C) Decrease assets, decrease equity

Explanation: Declaring a dividend reduces equity (Retained Earnings) and increases liabilities (Dividends Payable). When the dividend is paid, it decreases assets (Cash) and decreases liabilities (Dividends Payable). The net effect of the full cycle is a decrease in assets and a decrease in equity.

181. (Corporate Accounting)
Which of the following is true about preferred stock?
A. It always has voting rights
B. It ranks above common stock in dividends and liquidation
C. It is recorded in a liability account
D. It must be issued before common stock

Answer: B) It ranks above common stock in dividends and liquidation

Explanation: A key feature of preferred stock is that it has priority (seniority) over common stock in the payment of dividends and in the distribution of assets during liquidation.

182. (Accounts Payable)
A schedule of accounts payable is used to:
A. List all vendors and the amounts owed to them
B. Reconcile bank statements
C. Track customer payments
D. Determine tax liabilities

Answer: A) List all vendors and the amounts owed to them

Explanation: A schedule of accounts payable is a listing of all vendors (creditors) in the accounts payable subsidiary ledger and the amount owed to each.
