73. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) The motion “Withdraw a Motion” is:
A) Always undebatable and requires a second
B) Debatable only when made after the motion is stated
C) Allowed only before debate has begun
D) Allowed by unanimous consent if not yet seconded

Answer: D) Allowed by unanimous consent if not yet seconded

Explanation: If a motion has not been stated by the chair, the maker can withdraw it without any formal process. After it is stated, withdrawal requires permission of the assembly, usually granted by unanimous consent.

74. (FBLA Bylaws) The national parliamentarian is selected by:
A) General election of members
B) The Board of Directors
C) Appointment by the president with approval of the officer screening committee
D) The national officers’ majority vote

Answer: B) The Board of Directors

Explanation: The FBLA Board of Directors is responsible for appointing the national parliamentarian.

75. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) Which motion allows members to request information directly related to business at hand?
A) Point of Order
B) Parliamentary Inquiry
C) Division
D) Question of Privilege

Answer: B) Parliamentary Inquiry

Explanation: A Parliamentary Inquiry is a request for the chair's opinion or information on a matter of parliamentary procedure as it relates to the current business.

76. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) A “friendly amendment”:
A) Is adopted without a vote
B) Must still be formally moved, seconded, and voted upon
C) Automatically passes if agreed to by the maker of the motion
D) Can only be made by the presiding officer

Answer: B) Must still be formally moved, seconded, and voted upon

Explanation: There is no such thing as an informal "friendly amendment" in parliamentary procedure. Any change to a motion must follow the formal process of moving, seconding, and adopting an amendment.

77. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) If a quorum is lost during a meeting:
A) All decisions made before remain valid
B) Voting on motions already in debate may continue
C) New motions may still be made
D) Debate continues but no votes may be taken

Answer: A) All decisions made before remain valid

Explanation: Business transacted while a quorum was present remains valid. However, once a quorum is lost, no new business can be transacted, and no votes can be taken.

78. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) The only motion that can be made while the Previous Question is pending is:
A) Adjourn
B) Amend
C) Reconsider
D) Recess

Answer: D) Recess

Explanation: The motion for a Recess is a privileged motion and has a higher precedence than the subsidiary motion for the Previous Question, so it is in order while the Previous Question is pending.

79. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) Which of the following is always out of order?
A) Lay on the Table
B) Call for the Orders of the Day
C) Reconsider
D) Main Motion when another has the floor

Answer: D) Main Motion when another has the floor

Explanation: It is always out of order to introduce a new main motion when another member has been assigned the floor (is speaking).

80. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) Which motion is used when the assembly wishes to meet again to continue the business of the current meeting?
A) Postpone Definitely
B) Fix the Time to Which to Adjourn
C) Recess
D) Adjourn Sine Die

Answer: B) Fix the Time to Which to Adjourn

Explanation: This motion sets up an adjourned meeting—a continuation of the current meeting at a later time. It is different from scheduling a new, regular meeting.

81. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) Which of the following motions is never debatable?
A) Commit
B) Amend
C) Reconsider
D) Previous Question

Answer: D) Previous Question

Explanation: The motion for the Previous Question ("to close debate and vote immediately") is itself not debatable.

82. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) “Orders of the Day” can be set aside by:
A) Majority vote
B) Two-thirds vote
C) Chair’s discretion
D) Motion to Lay on the Table

Answer: B) Two-thirds vote

Explanation: Setting aside the Orders of the Day (the agenda) requires suspending the rules, which needs a two-thirds vote.

83. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) Which is a main motion that brings a question back before the assembly?
A) Amend
B) Reconsider
C) Take from the Table
D) Appeal

Answer: C) Take from the Table

Explanation: Take from the Table is a main motion that resumes consideration of a motion that was previously laid on the table.

84. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) Which of these is an example of a question of privilege?
A) "Can we suspend the rules?"
B) "Can we take a five-minute break?"
C) "I can’t hear the speaker."
D) "I object to consideration of the motion."

Answer: C) "I can’t hear the speaker."

Explanation: A Question of Privilege is a request dealing with the comfort, safety, or rights of the assembly or its members. An inability to hear is a classic example.

85. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) The motion to “Divide the Question” is used when:
A) There is a tie
B) A member disagrees with part of a motion
C) The motion contains unrelated parts that should be voted on separately
D) The chair refuses to recognize a motion

Answer: C) The motion contains unrelated parts that should be voted on separately

Explanation: Division of a Question is used to split a motion with multiple independent proposals into individual motions for separate voting.

86. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) Which of the following cannot interrupt a speaker?
A) Point of Order
B) Parliamentary Inquiry
C) Main Motion
D) Division

Answer: C) Main Motion

Explanation: A main motion is the lowest-ranking motion and must wait until the current speaker has finished and the floor is free.

87. (FBLA Bylaws) How many regions is FBLA divided into at the national level?
A) Four
B) Five
C) Six
D) Seven

Answer: B) Five

Explanation: FBLA is divided into five national regions: Western, Mountain Plains, Central, Southern, and Eastern.

88. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) What type of vote is required to adopt the motion “Limit Debate”?
A) Majority
B) Plurality
C) Two-thirds
D) Three-fourths

Answer: C) Two-thirds

Explanation: Because it restricts the rights of members to speak, the motion to Limit Debate requires a two-thirds vote.

89. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) When a main motion is being debated, which motion is out of order?
A) Commit
B) Recess
C) Adjourn
D) Take from the Table

Answer: D) Take from the Table

Explanation: Take from the Table is used to resume a tabled motion. It is not in order when another main motion is already on the floor and being debated.

90. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) “Amend Something Previously Adopted” requires:
A) Majority vote with notice
B) Two-thirds vote or majority with notice
C) Majority of those present
D) Vote of all national officers

Answer: B) Two-thirds vote or majority with notice

Explanation: To change a decision already made, the vote required is either a two-thirds vote, a majority of the entire membership, or a majority vote with previous notice given at a prior meeting.

91. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) Which of the following motions is not allowed when a motion to Reconsider is pending?
A) Postpone Definitely
B) Amend
C) Lay on the Table
D) Second Reconsider

Answer: D) Second Reconsider

Explanation: You cannot move to Reconsider a vote on the motion to Reconsider itself. Only one motion to Reconsider can be pending on the same question.

92. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) Who decides questions of order when there is no existing precedent?
A) Majority of the assembly
B) The Parliamentarian
C) The Chair
D) The Secretary

Answer: C) The Chair

Explanation: The presiding officer is responsible for making initial rulings on all questions of order. The parliamentarian only advises; the chair decides.

93. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) The term “floor” refers to:
A) Physical space in the assembly hall
B) The speaker’s podium
C) The right to speak or present business
D) The stage reserved for officers

Answer: C) The right to speak or present business

Explanation: Having the "floor" means a member has been recognized by the chair and has the exclusive right to speak.

94. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) A motion is considered out of order when:
A) It conflicts with bylaws
B) It has not been seconded
C) Debate has already started
D) The maker is not a voting member

Answer: A) It conflicts with bylaws

Explanation: A motion that conflicts with the organization's bylaws or constitution is always out of order. The other options may be procedural issues but do not automatically make a motion out of order.

95. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) Which motion is in order when the motion to Adjourn is pending?
A) Postpone Indefinitely
B) Suspend the Rules
C) Fix the Time to Which to Adjourn
D) Commit

Answer: C) Fix the Time to Which to Adjourn

Explanation: Fix the Time to Which to Adjourn is a privileged motion with higher precedence than Adjourn, so it is in order even when Adjourn is pending.

96. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) A member may demand a roll call vote if:
A) The bylaws require it
B) The presiding officer allows it
C) Any single member requests it
D) The group adopts it by motion

Answer: A) The bylaws require it

Explanation: A roll call vote is not a right of a single member unless the organization's bylaws or rules specifically grant that right. It is usually ordered by a majority vote of the assembly.

97. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) Which of the following motions is used when a member believes the rules of the organization are being violated?
A) Parliamentary Inquiry
B) Division
C) Point of Order
D) Previous Question

Answer: C) Point of Order

Explanation: A Point of Order is the proper motion to call attention to a violation of the rules.

98. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) Which of the following actions results in final disposal of the main motion?
A) Refer to a Committee
B) Postpone to a Certain Time
C) Lay on the Table
D) Postpone Indefinitely

Answer: D) Postpone Indefinitely

Explanation: Of these options, only Postpone Indefinitely finally disposes of the main motion for the session. The others are temporary dispositions.

99. (FBLA Bylaws) FBLA’s national president must be elected from among candidates representing:
A) Any region
B) Western or Southern regions
C) The Eastern region only
D) Each state must rotate annually

Answer: A) Any region

Explanation: The FBLA national president can be elected from any of the five national regions; there is no regional restriction for this office.

100. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) The chair's declaration of “the ayes have it” can be challenged by:
A) Motion to Suspend the Rules
B) Point of Order
C) Division
D) Reconsider

Answer: C) Division

Explanation: A Division of the Assembly is the specific motion used to challenge the chair's announcement of the result of a voice vote.
