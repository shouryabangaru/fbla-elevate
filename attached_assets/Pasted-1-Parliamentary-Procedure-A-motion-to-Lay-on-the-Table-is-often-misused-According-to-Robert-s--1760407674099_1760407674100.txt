1. (Parliamentary Procedure)
A motion to “Lay on the Table” is often misused. According to Robert's Rules, its legitimate purpose is to:
A) Kill a motion without debate
B) Delay action until the next regular meeting
C) Temporarily set aside business for more urgent matters
D) Refer the motion to a committee

Answer: C) Temporarily set aside business for more urgent matters

Explanation: The correct use of "Lay on the Table" is to temporarily set aside a pending motion to deal with an immediate, more urgent piece of business, with the intent to "Take from the Table" and resume consideration later in the same session.

3. (Parliamentary Procedure)
If a member rises to a point of order, when must the presiding officer respond?
A) Immediately, unless another member is speaking
B) After the current speaker finishes
C) At the end of debate
D) Only if a vote is being taken

Answer: A) Immediately, unless another member is speaking

Explanation: A Point of Order deals with a breach of the rules and is therefore a privileged interruption. The chair must rule on it immediately when it is raised, unless another member is speaking, in which case the point is addressed as soon as the speaker finishes.

4. (Parliamentary Procedure)

C) A member who voted on the prevailing side
D) The chairperson

Answer: C) A member who voted on the prevailing side

Explanation: A fundamental rule for the motion to Reconsider is that it must be made by a member who voted on the winning side of the original vote.
