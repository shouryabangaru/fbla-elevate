1. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) When a motion is postponed definitely, it may:
A) Be renewed at the same session once it is postponed
B) Be laid on the table without debate when it is resumed
C) Be reconsidered only if debate has not occurred
D) Resume at the time specified without further action

Answer: D) Resume at the time specified without further action

Explanation: The motion to Postpone to a Certain Time (or "definitely") schedules the main motion to come back automatically at the specified time. No new motion is needed to resume its consideration.

2. (FBLA Bylaws) According to the FBLA National Bylaws, which of the following is not a required officer for a local chapter?
A) Historian
B) President
C) Secretary
D) Treasurer

Answer: A) Historian

Explanation: While many chapters have a Historian, the FBLA National Bylaws only require a chapter to have, at a minimum, a President, Secretary, and Treasurer.

3. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) In the absence of a quorum, a deliberative assembly may:
A) Adopt main motions requiring only a simple majority
B) Approve routine reports
C) Adjourn or take measures to obtain a quorum
D) Refer items to a committee

Answer: C) Adjourn or take measures to obtain a quorum

Explanation: Without a quorum, an assembly cannot conduct substantive business. The only motions in order are those related to obtaining a quorum (like a Recess) or to Adjourn (except to adjourn to a later time).

4. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) If a motion to “Reconsider” is made while another item is pending:
A) It is taken up immediately
B) It is debatable only if the motion it reconsiders is debatable
C) It is treated as incidental
D) It is postponed automatically

Answer: C) It is treated as incidental

Explanation: When a motion to Reconsider is made while other business is pending, it is classified as an incidental motion and must be decided immediately before the pending business can continue.

6. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) The motion to "Commit or Refer" can be applied to:
A) Privileged motions
B) Main motions and some amendments
C) Only main motions
D) Main and incidental motions

Answer: B) Main motions and some amendments

Explanation: The subsidiary motion to Commit or Refer can be applied to a main motion and any amendments that are pending with it. It cannot be applied to other types of motions.

7. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) An amendment to an amendment:
A) Must be germane to the primary amendment
B) Is debatable only if the main motion is
C) Requires a two-thirds vote
D) Can introduce a new subject

Answer: A) Must be germane to the primary amendment

Explanation: A secondary amendment (amendment to an amendment) must be directly related to and within the scope of the primary amendment it seeks to change.

8. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) Which of the following is true about the motion to “Suspend the Rules”?
A) It is not allowed under any circumstances to suspend bylaws
B) It requires a majority vote under all conditions
C) It allows temporary violation of rules set by the bylaws
D) It may be applied to standing rules and parliamentary law

Answer: D) It may be applied to standing rules and parliamentary law

Explanation: The motion to Suspend the Rules can be used to temporarily set aside standing rules (rules of order) or an ordinary rule of the assembly. It cannot be used to suspend bylaws or fundamental principles of parliamentary law, and it requires a two-thirds vote.

10. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) A Point of Order can be ruled upon by:
A) The maker of the motion
B) The majority vote of the body
C) The presiding officer, unless appealed
D) The parliamentarian directly

Answer: C) The presiding officer, unless appealed

Explanation: The chair makes an initial ruling on a Point of Order. This ruling can be appealed to the assembly for a final decision, but the chair's ruling stands unless overturned by a majority vote.

11. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) Which of the following motions can be renewed after it has been defeated, provided no other motion of the same nature is pending?
A) Limit Debate
B) Lay on the Table
C) Postpone Indefinitely
D) Previous Question

Answer: A) Limit Debate

Explanation: Motions that do not conflict with a motion previously defeated can be renewed after progress in debate or business. Subsidiary motions like Limit Debate can often be renewed, unlike Lay on the Table.

12. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) “Fix the Time to Which to Adjourn” is:
A) A main motion
B) An incidental motion
C) A privileged motion of highest rank
D) A subsidiary motion

Answer: C) A privileged motion of highest rank

Explanation: Fix the Time to Which to Adjourn is the highest-ranking privileged motion. It sets up a future meeting before the current one is adjourned and is in order even when other business is pending.

14. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) If a member "calls for the orders of the day," the chair must:
A) Ask for unanimous consent to continue current business
B) Take an immediate vote on whether to proceed
C) Automatically revert to the scheduled order
D) Rule whether the call is appropriate

Answer: C) Automatically revert to the scheduled order

Explanation: A Call for the Orders of the Day is a demand to follow the adopted agenda or order of business. The chair must immediately comply unless the assembly votes by a two-thirds majority to set the orders aside.

15. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) The chair votes:
A) Only when there is a tie
B) Always as the last vote cast
C) When their vote would affect the outcome
D) Only on motions requiring a two-thirds vote

Answer: C) When their vote would affect the outcome

Explanation: The presiding officer usually does not vote to maintain impartiality. They only vote when their vote would change the outcome (e.g., to break a tie or to create a tie to defeat a motion requiring a majority).

16. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) Which of the following is not a debatable privileged motion?
A) Fix the Time to Which to Adjourn
B) Raise a Question of Privilege
C) Recess
D) Call for the Orders of the Day

Answer: D) Call for the Orders of the Day

Explanation: Call for the Orders of the Day is not debatable; it is a demand to follow the schedule. The other privileged motions (Fix the Time..., Question of Privilege, Recess) can be debatable under certain circumstances.

17. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) The motion to “Postpone Indefinitely” is primarily used to:
A) Delay a vote until a later meeting
B) End consideration of a question without a direct vote
C) Suspend the main motion for further amendment
D) Move discussion into executive session

Answer: B) End consideration of a question without a direct vote

Explanation: Postpone Indefinitely is used to test the assembly's support for a main motion without taking a direct vote on it. If it passes, the main motion is dismissed for the session.

A) The members are removed from office
B) Its duties are suspended temporarily
C) Its responsibilities are concluded or reassigned
D) Its membership must be reaffirmed

Answer: C) Its responsibilities are concluded or reassigned

Explanation: To discharge a committee means to end its assignment and take the matter back into the hands of the assembly, concluding the committee's work on that task.

20. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) Which of the following is not true about subsidiary motions?
A) They are always applied to main motions
B) They can have higher precedence than main motions
C) They are always debatable
D) They modify or dispose of a pending motion

Answer: C) They are always debatable

Explanation: Subsidiary motions are not always debatable. For example, the motion for the Previous Question (to close debate) is itself not debatable.

21. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) An appeal is:
A) Decided by the presiding officer
B) Debatable in all cases
C) Not in order when another appeal is pending
D) Only allowed if the ruling was made by majority vote

Answer: C) Not in order when another appeal is pending

Explanation: Only one appeal may be pending at a time. An appeal is debatable only if the motion it relates to was debatable, and it is decided by the assembly, not the chair.

22. (FBLA Bylaws) In FBLA, the duties of the parliamentarian include:

B) Presiding at all meetings
C) Advising on the constitutionality of motions

Answer: C) Advising on the constitutionality of motions

Explanation: The parliamentarian is a consultant to the chair on procedural matters, advising on rules and the proper interpretation of the bylaws and parliamentary authority.

23. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) If a motion requires a two-thirds vote and only a voice vote is taken, any member may:
A) Demand a roll call
B) Request a division
C) Move to table the motion
D) Suspend the voting requirements

Answer: B) Request a division

Explanation: A Division of the Assembly is a demand for a more precise vote (e.g., a rising vote) when the result of a voice vote is unclear or doubted. This is crucial for motions requiring a two-thirds vote.

24. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) A “Division of a Question” differs from “Division of the Assembly” in that it:
A) Requires a majority vote
B) Requires the presiding officer’s ruling
C) Applies to visual vote discrepancies
D) Separates a motion into parts for consideration

Answer: D) Separates a motion into parts for consideration

Explanation: Division of a Question is used to split a motion with multiple independent parts into separate motions for individual voting. Division of the Assembly is used to verify a vote count.

26. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) If a motion is “laid on the table,” what must occur for it to be taken up again?

B) A motion to resume debate at any time

D) A unanimous vote of those present

Answer: C) A majority vote to take from the table within the same session or next

Explanation: The motion to Take from the Table is used to resume consideration of a tabled motion. It requires a second and a majority vote and is only in order during the same session or the next regular session.

27. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) In the standard order of precedence, which motion outranks “Amend”?
A) Commit or Refer
B) Main Motion
C) Reconsider
D) Postpone Indefinitely

Answer: A) Commit or Refer

Explanation: In the standard order of precedence, subsidiary motions rank as follows (highest to lowest): Lay on the Table, Previous Question, Limit/Extend Debate, Postpone to a Certain Time, Commit or Refer, Amend, Postpone Indefinitely.

C) Appeal the ruling of the chair
D) Call the previous question

Answer: C) Appeal the ruling of the chair

Explanation: If a member disagrees with the chair's ruling on a Point of Order, they can immediately "appeal from the decision of the chair," which puts the final decision to a majority vote of the assembly.

31. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) The motion “Reconsider and Enter on the Minutes” is used when:

Answer: C) A member wants to reserve the right to reconsider at a later time

Explanation: This special form of the motion to Reconsider is made to prevent a temporary majority from taking action and then adjourning before the minority can muster votes to reconsider. It delays the actual reconsideration vote until the next meeting.

Answer: C) Suppress the main motion for the duration of the session

Explanation: Adopting Postpone Indefinitely kills the main motion for the entire session. It cannot be brought up again during that same meeting.

C) To postpone indefinitely
D) To suspend standing rules

Answer: B) To limit or extend limits of debate

Explanation: Motions that restrict the rights of the assembly, such as limiting debate (Previous Question or Limit Debate), require a two-thirds vote because they take away the right of free and full discussion.

36. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) “Lay on the Table” is often misused to:

C) Refer a motion to a committee

Answer: B) Kill a motion without voting

Explanation: Lay on the Table is meant to temporarily set aside a motion to deal with more urgent business. It is often misused as a way to kill a motion because if it is not taken from the table, it effectively dies.

37. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) The motion “Fix the Time to Which to Adjourn” is classified as:

C) An incidental main motion

Answer: B) A privileged motion of the highest rank

Explanation: This motion is the highest-ranking privileged motion. It is in order even when other business is pending because it relates to the continuation of the assembly itself.

B) Rise to a Question of Privilege
C) Appeal from the decision of the chair
D) Request a Division

Answer: C) Appeal from the decision of the chair

Explanation: The proper recourse when a member disagrees with a ruling from the chair is to make an Appeal, which allows the assembly to make the final decision by majority vote.

72. (Parliamentary Procedure Principles) The motion “Object to the Consideration of the Question” must be made:
A) Before any debate has occurred
B) After an amendment is adopted
C) At any time during discussion
D) During committee of the whole

Answer: A) Before any debate has occurred

Explanation: This motion must be made immediately after the main motion is stated by the chair and before any debate or subsidiary motion has been made.
